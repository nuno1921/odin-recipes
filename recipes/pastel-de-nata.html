Pastel de Nata

Prep Time: 1h 20 mins

Cook Time: 15 mins

Total Time: 1 h 45 min

Servings: 12

Pastel de Natas, also known as Portuguese custard tarts, are a delectable treat that showcases the artistry of Portuguese pastry-making. These delightful pastries consist of a flaky and buttery pastry crust filled with a smooth and creamy egg custard. With a golden-brown, slightly caramelized top, the Pastel de Natas offers a delicate balance of flavors. The custard filling is rich, subtly sweet, and infused with a hint of vanilla. The contrast between the crispy pastry and the luscious custard creates a delightful textural experience. Originating from the historic city of Lisbon, Pastel de Natas have gained international fame and are cherished as a symbol of Portuguese culinary heritage. Whether enjoyed with a cup of coffee or as a sweet ending to a meal, these tarts offer a taste of Portugal's pastry mastery and are sure to leave you longing for more.

Pastel de nata:

    Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon (for dusting)

Step by Step:

    Prepare the pastry:

  1. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F) and lightly grease a muffin tin or use individual tart molds.
  2. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface until it becomes a thin rectangle.
  3. Starting from the shorter end, tightly roll up the pastry into a log. Cut the log into small slices, about 1 centimeter thick. Press each slice into the bottom and up the sides of the muffin tin or tart molds, forming the pastry crusts.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar and flour. Add the egg yolks to the sugar mixture and whisk until well combined and smooth.
  5. Gradually pour the warm milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
  6. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring continuously, until it thickens and comes to a gentle boil. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  7. Allow the custard filling to cool for a few minutes, then pour it into the prepared pastry crusts.
  8. Bake the Pastel de Natas in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes or until the pastry is golden and the custard is set with a slight jiggle in the center.
  9. Remove the tarts from the oven and let them cool in the tin or molds for a few minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Once cooled, dust the Pastel de Natas with ground cinnamon.
  11. Serve the Pastel de Natas warm or at room temperature and enjoy the delicious taste of these Portuguese custard tarts!

Note: For an extra touch, you can sprinkle a little powdered sugar on top or use a kitchen torch to caramelize the tops of the custard before dusting with cinnamon.
